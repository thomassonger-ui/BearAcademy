WHAT THE BROKERAGE PROVIDES

Five resources Bear Team gives every agent. Click any row to see what happens when an agent skips it.

BearTeamOS™
Centralized operational platform — pipeline, tasks, communication, files.
▾
IF YOU SKIP IT

If you shadow-track in personal tools (phone notes, sticky notes, your memory), the broker loses the ability to see and support your deals. Every "I forgot" or "I lost track" becomes invisible until it's too late to fix. The platform exists so the team can operate as a team, not as a loose collection of solo operators.

Defined Transaction Workflows
Every deal follows structured milestones from lead to close.
▾
IF YOU SKIP IT

Invent your own deal flow and your timeline breaks. Inspection windows close. Appraisal contingencies expire. Missed milestones are the #1 driver of failed transactions and unhappy clients. The workflow exists because memory and instinct are not durable enough to manage 6–12 simultaneous deadlines per deal.

Brand Positioning
Consistent market presence that builds trust and credibility.
▾
IF YOU SKIP IT

If your listing flyers, signs, and emails are off-brand, you erase the brokerage's market presence and replace it with your own DIY look. Clients trust the brand they recognize, not the agent who designed something new at midnight. Brand consistency is what makes referrals and repeat business compound.

Administrative Support
Operational support so agents focus on production, not paperwork.
▾
IF YOU SKIP IT

Bypass admin support and you lose 3–5 hours per deal you should have spent on revenue activity. The math: that's at least one full deal per quarter you don't close. Admin isn't a chore the brokerage is offloading on you — it's a force multiplier the brokerage is offering you. Use it.

Compliance Oversight
Broker-level review that protects the agent and the brokerage.
▾
IF YOU SKIP IT

Skip compliance review and you carry the legal exposure alone. The broker's review exists to catch what you can't see — undisclosed dual-agency risk, unsigned addenda, missing required disclosures. Skipping it doesn't speed you up. It sets you up.
